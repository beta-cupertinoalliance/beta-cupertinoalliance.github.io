About the Cupertino Alliance

A micronational organization committed to the development and improvement of member nations.

There are many organizations in the micronational community. So many in fact, they are often referred to as "YAMO"; "Yet Another Micronational Organization". They often exhibit the failure of identifying problems in the community, many of which their members contribute to.

What makes the Cupertino Alliance different, is that we work to stop the ever-growing problem of inactivity within the micronational community.

We aren't just another micronational organization, because the CA makes every effort to be the micronational organization. We speak sense, and respect your opinion. We have access to useful agreements, documents and resources. Everything we have put into place is for our members. We are a rising star in the micronational community.

MEMBERS

Members gain access to all programs, get to run for Chair, Lieutenant Chair, Superior Judge, Associate Judge, and ministerial positions, as well as be allowed to appoint 3 delegates.

4

OBSERVERS

Observers do not have access to programs nor are allowed to run for positions, but are allowed to appoint 3 delegates to report to Parliament.

130+

SESSIONS

All delegates of the Cupertino Alliance report to Parliament in a session, where delegates discuss Cupertino Alliance legislation, issues, new programs, and the like.

64

DELEGATES

Delegates represent their nation that they were appointed by, report to Parliament where they vote and discuss on issues and legislature, and can participate in Alliance events, run for positions, etc.

Cupertino Alliance

The Cupertino Alliance is an intermicronational organization founded on March 10, 2019. Headquartered in Point Cupertino, Aenderia, it currently has 36 member states and 5 observer states.
